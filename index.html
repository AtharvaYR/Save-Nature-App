Desert Region

  1. Deserts are dry, barren areas with very little plant or animal life.
  2. They are found in hot, dry climates.
  3. There are also cold deserts, which are found in high altitudes or near the poles.
  4. Plants like Cardón cacti are found in the Baja California Desert and Cataviña region, Mexico.

Tundra Region

  1. The tundra is a cold, treeless region found in the Arctic.
  2. It is defined by its low temperatures and short growing season.
  3. The tundra is home to some of the world’s hardiest plants and animals, including mosses, lichens, and small shrubs.
  4. Plants like short shrubs, sedges, grasses, flowers, birch trees and willow trees are also found in the Tundra regions.

Taiga Region

  1. The taiga is a large forest that stretches across much of Siberia and northern North America.
  2. It is defined by its coniferous trees (evergreens) and its cool climate.
  3. Plants like birches, alders, aspens, willows, poplars, and rowans are found in the Taiga regions.

Tropical Rainforest

  • The tropical rainforest is a hot, moist environment found near the Earth’s equator.
  • It is defined by its dense vegetation and high rainfall.
  • Plants like Orchids, Philodendrons, Ferns, Bromeliads, Kapok Trees, Banana Trees, Rubber Trees, Bam- boo, Trees, Cassava Trees, Avocado Trees are found in the Tropical rainforest.

Temperate Forest

  1. The temperate forest is a moderate environment found in the mid-latitudes.
  2. It is defined by its deciduous trees (trees that lose their leaves in winter) and its moderate climate.
  3. Plants like Lichen, moss, ferns, wildflowers and other small plants are found in the Temperate forest.

Grassland Region

  • Grasslands are areas of open land with grasses and other small plants.
  • They are found in the mid-latitudes and are defined by their grassy vegetation.
  • Various species of grasses like purple needlegrass, blue grama, buffalo grass, and galleta are found in the Grassland region.
